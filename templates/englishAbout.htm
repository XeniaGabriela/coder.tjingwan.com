My Personal Relation to Coding

I studied mathematics at the Leibniz University of Hanover. After finishing my diploma, which is roughly equivalent to a master, I worked at the Institute of Algebraic Geometry for some years. There I researched for the DFG Priority Project SPP 1489 with the title "Algorithmic and Experimental Methods in Algebra, Geometry and Number Theory". The major part of my work was to translate complex algorithms from mathematical theory into code.
During this time, I implemented two libraries into the Computer Algebra System Singular. One was for the normalization of arithmetic surfaces and the other for Lipmans Desingularization Algorithm, which applies to surfaces over the integers or over a field. You can take a look into the latter library reslipman.lib here.
That was when I discovered my passion for coding. So I decided to look for software development positions in the industry.
